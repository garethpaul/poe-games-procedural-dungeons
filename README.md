# Procedural Dungeon

A dungeon forges itself in front of you — rooms scatter, wire into corridors,
and rise as a torch-lit 3D miniature — and then you play it. Tap anywhere to
send your adventurer walking: enemies you pass strike you but drop gold,
chests are pure treasure, blue shrines heal you once, and the glowing red
crystal at the far end is the boss. Reach it alive to clear the floor and bank
your haul; every floor cleared forges a deeper one.

Bring friends: everyone in the room explores the **same floor at the same
time**. You see their heroes walking beside yours, and the world is
first-come-first-served — a chest they open is gone, an elite they slay is
dead, and the first player to fell the boss ends the race for everyone. All
gold banks to the room leaderboard.

Anyone can reforge the floor from the ⚒ panel — pick a seed and one of five
themes (Ancient, Molten, Frost, Grim, Verdant), or watch the generation
pipeline build it stage by stage with the graph overlay on. Route planning is
the game: HP doesn't come back on its own, so a greedy detour through a
treasure vault might leave too little blood for the boss.

Ported from the open-source [Dungeon Forge](https://github.com/majidmanzarpour/threejs-procedural-dungeon)
generator by [Majid Manzarpour](https://x.com/majidmanzarpour) (MIT license),
with an original playable crawl layer built for Poe.
